US & Canada Cities: Marching South 2015-2100

Another visualization based on US & Canada global climate matches, following the recent interactive map. This time, we're focusing on the change between matches in 2015, 2050 and 2100, using NASA NEX-GDDP climate data for the future predictions.

This parallel coordinates chart uses changes in color to highlight the net change between matches for the years 2015 and 2100. The first column (i.e. the first item on the horizontal x-axis) represents the actual distances from the equator of the US or Canadian city. Columns 2-4 represent the distance from the equator for climate matches for 2015, 2050 and 2100.

Red lines represent more a match closer to the equator in 2100 than 2015, and blue lines represent one more distant from the equator. Red lines clearly dominate the chart, illustrating a net shift towards the equator in US and Canadian climate analogs. This southward shift is quantified by mean figures in the bar chart at the bottom: all latitude bands show a mean shift towards the south.

To navigate matches for all of the cities in the global data set, check out the similar.city map.
