Inverted US & Canada Cities: Global Climate Matches 2015-2100

The interactive data visualization below shows global cities that have similar weather to cities in the United States and Canada. The matches are based on the climate matching algorithm underpinning this site and cover the years 2015, 2050 and 2100. All matches are for global cities outside of the US & Canada. A few cities in northern Mexico have been excluded from this because they are geographically close to the cities in the southern US.

The 2050 and 2100 matches are based on the NASA NEX-GDDP climate data set with the CESM1.0 climate model with the RCP 8.5 greenhouse gas emissions scenario.

In essence, North American cities will have climatically shifted 309km (192 miles) to the south by the year 2100.
From the NASA climate forecast data, the effects of climate change are clear: you can see that American and Canadian cities are matching warmer places as time progresses. In effect, we are seeing North American cities march southward - the average distance from the equator of matches in 2015 is 4,793km. By 2100, the mean distance from the equator of matching cities is 4,484km. In essence, North American cities will have climatically shifted 309km (192 miles) to the south by the year 2100.

Click on the 'Share of matches' bubble chart to filter the maps. That bubble chart shows the proportion of US & Canada city matches in 2015 per global country. The filter applies to the 2015, 2050 and 2100 maps, although the filtered cities are based on on the 2015 map.

To navigate matches for all of the cities in the global data set, check out the similar.city map.
